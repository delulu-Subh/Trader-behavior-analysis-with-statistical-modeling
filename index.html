08 · Key Insights
Behavioral Findings & Pattern Discovery

Data-driven behavioral patterns uncovered from the full analysis pipeline.

📉
Fear Outperforms Greed
Traders earn $126/trade during Fear vs only $46 during Extreme Greed. Fear regimes self-select high-conviction traders — the weak hands exit, leaving disciplined operators who capture the bounce.
⚠️
Overconfidence in Greed
Trade size is largest during Fear ($8,857) — not Greed — but win rate drops to 76% in Greed. During Greed, more traders enter but with poor timing, crowding the market.
🔄
Contrarian Paradox
Longs during Extreme Greed yield 2.59% ROI vs 1.71% in Extreme Fear (p<0.0001). On perpetuals, momentum during greed phases is real — blind contrarianism is penalised on this venue.
🎯
Short Side Dominates in Fear
Short trades during Fear produce 6.67% ROI avg — the highest of any sentiment/direction combination. Profitable shorting during downturns is the primary alpha source for top-tier traders.
👑
Elite Trader Consistency
Top 20% traders maintain >93% win rate across all sentiments. The bottom 80% swing from 73% to 87%. Elite traders' edge is regime-independent — a hallmark of systematic, not emotional, trading.
📊
Greed Flips Positioning
Long ratio drops from 66.7% in Extreme Fear to 41.4% in Greed. The crowd becomes net short at market tops — indicating sophisticated crowd-fading behaviour among this cohort of traders.

10 · Limitations
Caveats & Scope Boundaries

Honest assessment of what this analysis can and cannot conclude.

Small Trader Sample
Only 32 unique traders — results may not generalise to broader market populations or retail participants.
No Leverage Data
The dataset lacks leverage per-trade, preventing a true leveraged-exposure analysis. Sharpe proxy is a substitute.
Survivorship Bias
Traders in this dataset are still active — losing traders who blew up accounts are not represented.
ML Data Leakage
Random Forest achieves 100% because ROI directly determines win_flag. The LR result (91.75%) is the more meaningful signal.
